---
title: Shipping While You're Still Learning
date: 2026-01-18
summary: A short essay about using public projects as both a portfolio and a feedback loop.
---

One of the easiest ways to stay stuck is to wait until you feel qualified before you publish anything.

The catch is that shipping is one of the fastest ways to become qualified.

When you put work in public, a few useful things happen at once. You are forced to finish decisions. You discover which parts of your understanding are still fuzzy. You also create a trail of proof that shows how you think, what you care about, and how quickly you improve.

That trail matters more than people think. A public project is not only a portfolio piece. It is a feedback loop.

You ship something small.

People react to it, ignore it, misunderstand it, or ask for more.

That response tells you what to sharpen next.

This is especially useful when you are still early in your career or changing directions. Instead of trying to present yourself as already finished, you can show momentum. Momentum is often more convincing than polish.

There is a difference between being careless and being in progress. Shipping while learning does not mean lowering standards. It means accepting that the standard gets higher *because* you keep shipping.

The internet is full of people waiting to feel ready. That is a crowded place to stay.
