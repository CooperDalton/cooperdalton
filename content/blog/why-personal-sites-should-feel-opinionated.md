---
title: Why Personal Sites Should Feel Opinionated
date: 2026-02-27
summary: On avoiding template energy and using interaction to communicate taste before text does.
---

A personal site is not just a container for information. It is also evidence of taste.

When every portfolio uses the same layout, the same animation library defaults, and the same section structure, the work starts to blur together. You can still communicate competence that way, but it is harder to communicate point of view.

The best personal sites feel like a small product made for one person: the creator. They make a few strong decisions and then commit to them.

That does not mean the site needs to be loud or complicated. It means the choices should feel intentional:

- Why does the page move the way it does?
- Why are these sections emphasized?
- Why this typography, spacing, or pacing?
- Why does the site feel like *you* instead of a framework demo?

Interaction is especially good at carrying this kind of signal. Before someone reads your bio, they have already felt how the page behaves. That is why motion, transitions, and structure matter so much on a portfolio. They are not decoration. They are part of the message.

I think the goal is to avoid "template energy." A site should feel authored. It should have constraints, preferences, and a little bit of personality. Even subtle decisions can do that if they are consistent.

An opinionated personal site does not need to impress everyone. It just needs to feel true to the person making it.
